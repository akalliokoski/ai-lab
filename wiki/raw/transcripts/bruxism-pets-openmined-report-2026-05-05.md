# Privacy-Enhancing Technologies for Bruxism Detection from EMG Data: An Intersection of JawSense (ONE) and OpenMined

Source type: user-provided research report pasted into the session on 2026-05-05.

High-level points preserved from the source:
- Bruxism is common and jaw EMG is simultaneously health data, behavioral data, and a likely biometric identifier.
- A JawSense-style wearable pipeline is a strong example of why raw EMG upload is privacy-sensitive.
- OpenMined/PySyft, Opacus, secure aggregation, SMPC, and HE form a layered privacy toolbox rather than a single silver bullet.
- A practical architecture is edge inference on the wearable/phone, federated training during charging, secure aggregation of updates, DP on gradients/statistics, and remote-data-science access via Datasites.
- FL for EMG has promising offline/open-loop evidence but also important limits for real-time closed-loop personalization.
- The strongest near-term research opportunities are not generic "use FL" claims but concrete questions around gradient leakage, utility/privacy trade-offs, sequential personalization, and regulatory/operational design.
