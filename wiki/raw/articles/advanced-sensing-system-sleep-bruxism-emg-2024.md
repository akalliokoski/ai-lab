# Advanced Sensing System for Sleep Bruxism across Multiple Postures via EMG and Machine Learning

Source: PubMed / Sensors (2024)
PMID: 39205120
PMCID: PMC11358964
DOI: 10.3390/s24165426

## Abstract-style summary
- Feasibility-style EMG study using 10 healthy subjects aged 18-25 with no prior history of bruxism.
- Collected temporalis and masseter EMG across supine and lateral recumbent postures.
- Extracted 10 time-domain features and compared six classical classifiers.
- Random forest reportedly performed best.
- Total classifier input was 3250 samples from 150 data records, with class imbalance handled using ADASYN.

## Important caveat
Subjects simulated bruxism-like behaviors rather than providing natural overnight bruxism events. This makes it a useful hardware/feature-engineering reference, but not the right first benchmark for clinical detection.
