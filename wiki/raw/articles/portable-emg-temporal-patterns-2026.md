# Temporal patterns of bruxism behaviors: multiple day recording with portable electromyography

Source: PubMed / Scientific Reports (2026)
PMID: 41495115
DOI: 10.1038/s41598-025-30704-z

## Abstract-style summary
- Recorded 72 hours of continuous portable EMG in 20 subjects.
- Found day-to-day and hour-to-hour variation patterns that differed between sleep grinding, sleep clenching, awake grinding, and awake clenching.
- Argued that three days may be a minimum viable monitoring period for clinically meaningful bruxism assessment.

## Takeaway for ai-lab
The newest literature pushes the field toward multi-day portable EMG monitoring rather than single-night offline classification. This strengthens the case for an EMG-first project shape, while also making clear that tiny one-night benchmarks are only partial proxies for the real problem.
