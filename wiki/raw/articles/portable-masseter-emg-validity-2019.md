# Validity of single-channel masseteric electromyography by using an ultraminiature wearable electromyographic device for diagnosis of sleep bruxism

Source: PubMed / Journal of Prosthodontic Research (2020 online, PMID record 2019)
PMID: 31085074
DOI: 10.1016/j.jpor.2019.04.003

## Abstract-style summary
- Compared a single-channel wearable masseter EMG device against reference-standard PSG with audio-video review.
- Included 20 volunteers with clinical sleep bruxism suspicion; 16 were diagnosed as bruxers by the PSG-AV reference.
- Reported strong correlation between wearable single-channel EMG counts and PSG-AV scoring.
- Reported an apparent optimal sleep-bruxer cutoff near 5.5 EMG episodes/hour in this cohort.

## Takeaway for ai-lab
This is one of the clearest signals that masseter EMG is the translationally relevant sensing modality for practical bruxism monitoring. But it is a small proprietary validation cohort, not an open benchmark dataset.
