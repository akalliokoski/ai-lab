# Polysomnographic scoring of sleep bruxism events is accurate even in the absence of video recording but unreliable with EMG-only setups

Source: Europe PMC / Sleep and Breathing (2020)
PMCID: PMC7426313
DOI: 10.1007/s11325-019-01915-2

## Abstract-style summary
- Compared seven PSG scoring setups against PSG with audio and video.
- Included 19 volunteers with one-night PSG recordings.
- PSG with audio remained close to the PSG+audio+video reference, but EMG-only setups were unreliable.

## Takeaway for ai-lab
This is the strongest warning against overclaiming from EMG-only labels. EMG is a good starter signal for modeling, but not a substitute for gold-standard labeling when evaluating clinical validity.
