# Electromyography Frequency Spectrum Is a Better Indicator of Sleep Bruxism Severity Related to Temporomandibular Disorder Pain Than Conventional Masticatory Muscle Activation and Bruxism Time Indices-A Pilot Study

Source: PubMed / Journal of Sleep Research (2026)
PMID: 41581017

## Abstract-style summary
- Retrospectively analyzed home polysomnography data from 44 participants with possible sleep bruxism.
- Compared conventional activity/time indices against EMG frequency-spectrum descriptors during masticatory muscle activity events.
- Participants with temporomandibular disorder pain showed lower median EMG frequencies and lower absolute power, while conventional event-count indices did not distinguish the groups.
- Suggests EMG spectral content may track clinically meaningful severity better than simple counts alone.

## Takeaway for ai-lab
This does not prove the CAP benchmark is clinically valid, but it is a good caution against treating frequency-style EMG descriptors as irrelevant just because the first honest benchmark is small. Some spectral information may matter more for translational phenotyping than crude episode totals.
