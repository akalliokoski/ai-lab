# Comparison of the occurrence of sleep bruxism under accustomed conditions at home and under polysomnography conditions in a sleep laboratory

Source: PubMed / Journal of Prosthodontic Research (2022)
PMID: 35095085
DOI: 10.2186/jpr.JPR_D_21_00219

## Abstract-style summary
- Used the same single-channel wearable masseter EMG device in home and laboratory conditions, with PSG-audio-video also present in the lab.
- Included 20 healthy student volunteers clinically diagnosed with bruxism.
- Found significantly fewer bruxism bursts and episodes in the sleep-lab setting than at home.
- Supports the idea that ecological home monitoring can differ materially from laboratory PSG nights.

## Takeaway for ai-lab
This supports the latest wearable-bruxism direction: multi-night home EMG may be more representative than one lab night. It also means that a CAP-style PSG benchmark is useful for reproducibility, but not a perfect proxy for real longitudinal home monitoring.
