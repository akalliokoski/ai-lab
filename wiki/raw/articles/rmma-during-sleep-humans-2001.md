# Rhythmic masticatory muscle activity during sleep in humans

Source: PubMed / Journal of Dental Research (2001)
PMID: 11332529
DOI: 10.1177/00220345010800020801

## Abstract-style summary
- Polysomnographic data from 82 normal subjects were compared with 33 sleep bruxers.
- RMMA episodes were defined as three or more consecutive bursts of masseter EMG activity, with or without tooth-grinding.
- Bruxers had about twice as many masseter muscle bursts per episode and higher-amplitude episodes than controls who still exhibited RMMA.

## Takeaway for ai-lab
This is the cleanest direct source-level rationale for a burst-density-within-episode feature. It says that the clinically interesting organization variable is not just whether bursts occur, but how densely they cluster inside an RMMA episode.
