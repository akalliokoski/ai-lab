# A case-control study on the effect of rhythmic masticatory muscle activity (RMMA) clusters on sleep fragmentation and severity of orofacial muscle pain in sleep bruxism

Source: PubMed / Journal of Sleep Research (2024)
PMID: 37859534
DOI: 10.1111/jsr.14072

## Abstract-style summary
- Secondary PSG analysis in 184 adults studied whether RMMA clusters, rather than just isolated events, relate to sleep fragmentation and orofacial muscle pain severity.
- The paper explicitly distinguishes RMMA single events, pairs, and clusters.
- Higher-RMMA-cluster burden was the event-structure surface under study, not just gross 30-second activity level.
