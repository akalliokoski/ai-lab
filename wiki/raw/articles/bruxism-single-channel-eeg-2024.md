# Classification of bruxism based on time-frequency and nonlinear features of single channel EEG

Source: Europe PMC / BMC Oral Health (2024)
PMCID: PMC10787956
DOI: 10.1186/s12903-024-03865-y

## Abstract-style summary
- Study used the PhysioNet CAP Sleep Database.
- The paper reports that only 2 bruxism patients and 4 healthy controls had compatible channel configurations for the chosen analysis.
- Features were extracted from REM-sleep epochs from a single bipolar EEG channel.
- The best reported channel was C4-P4 with sensitivity 95.59%, specificity 98.44%, accuracy 97.84%, and PPV 94.20% under five-fold cross-validation.

## Important caveat
This is useful as a reproducible starting benchmark, but the sample size is extremely small and the reported epoch-level cross-validation likely overstates real-world performance. Treat it as a reproduction baseline, not as evidence that single-channel EEG is solved for bruxism.
