# Effects of vibratory feedback stimuli through an oral appliance on sleep bruxism: A 14-week intervention trial

Source: PubMed / Journal of Dental Sciences (2024)
PMID: 39035344

## Abstract-style summary
- Followed 20 participants with definite sleep bruxism during 98 nights of home use of a custom oral appliance with a force-triggered vibrator.
- Used a 3-week adaptation period, 9-week stimulation period, and 2-week post-stimulation period.
- Bruxism-event duration decreased during the stimulation period and rose again after stimulation stopped.
- Indicates that home biofeedback devices can produce sustained behavioral effects over multiple weeks.

## Takeaway for ai-lab
This is another sign that the strongest translational direction is not just offline classification from one night of data. Wearable sensing is increasingly tied to longitudinal intervention and management, which is useful for future framing but does not yet give the repo a better open benchmark.
