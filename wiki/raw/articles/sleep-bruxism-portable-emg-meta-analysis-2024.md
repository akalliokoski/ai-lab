# Instrumental assessment of sleep bruxism: a systematic review and meta-analysis

Source: PubMed / Sleep Medicine Reviews (2024)
PMID: 38295573
DOI: 10.1016/j.smrv.2024.101906

## Abstract-style summary
- Reviewed 10 human studies comparing portable EMG devices against polysomnography (PSG), the reference standard for sleep bruxism assessment.
- Meta-analysis found portable EMG approaches can be useful, but the certainty of evidence is limited by methodological shortcomings and varying thresholds.
- The paper is important because it supports EMG as the practical sensing modality while also showing that validation quality is still uneven.

## Takeaway for ai-lab
EMG is the most practical signal family for a real future device, but benchmark design and labels matter more than squeezing model complexity out of tiny studies.
