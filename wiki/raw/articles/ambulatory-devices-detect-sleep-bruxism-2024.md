# Ambulatory devices to detect sleep bruxism: a narrative review

Source: PubMed / Australian Dental Journal (2024)
PMID: 39976111
DOI: 10.1111/adj.13057

## Abstract-style summary
- Reviews commercially available ambulatory devices for sleep-bruxism detection through December 2024.
- Frames sleep bruxism as masticatory muscle activity during sleep that can be rhythmic or non-rhythmic.
- Concludes that selected ambulatory devices are promising as preliminary screening tools for clinic and home use, but they do not replace PSG with audio-video recording.
