# Reduction of sleep bruxism events according to contingent electrical stimulus intensity

Source: PubMed / Journal of Oral Rehabilitation (2024)
PMID: 38831662

## Abstract-style summary
- Studied 20 probable sleep bruxers using a portable EMG recorder with contingent electrical stimulation biofeedback.
- Compared a baseline home-recording period without stimulation against a later period with stimulation.
- Higher tolerated stimulation intensity correlated with larger reductions in sleep bruxism events per hour and with improvements in jaw-muscle symptom ratings.
- Suggests closed-loop wearable EMG is relevant not only for detection but also for intervention-oriented management.

## Takeaway for ai-lab
This pushes the translational target beyond passive detection. It supports treating intervention-oriented wearable EMG as a future science direction, while also reinforcing that the current CAP task is still only a benchmark proxy rather than the endpoint.
