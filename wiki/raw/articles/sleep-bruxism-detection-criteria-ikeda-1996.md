# Criteria for the detection of sleep-associated bruxism in humans

Source: PubMed / Journal of Orofacial Pain (1996)
PMID: 9161232

## Abstract-style summary
- Home sleep recordings over 4 consecutive nights in 9 subjects with sleep bruxism complaints used surface masseter EMG plus ECG.
- The paper proposed automated sleep-bruxism event detection criteria using masseter activity above a minimum threshold of 3% of each subject's individually established MVC.
- It reports mean number and duration of detected bruxism events and makes explicit that subject-specific thresholds are physiologically central to event detection.

## Takeaway for ai-lab
This is the clearest classical support for event-conditioned and subject-relative jaw-EMG summaries. Even early automated bruxism detection is framed around burst/event criteria and per-subject thresholds rather than only coarse fixed-window averages.
